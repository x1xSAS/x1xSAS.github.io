I am **Salman Abdullah Alsalman**, an academic professional, computer science lecturer, and AI consultant based in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia. My work focuses on the intersection of **Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, Computer Vision, and Computer Science education**.

I currently serve as a **University Lecturer in the Department of Computer Science at Prince Sattam bin Abdulaziz University**, where I teach and mentor students in programming, data structures, algorithms, web applications, and related computer science topics. Alongside my academic role, I am actively interested in helping organizations and small businesses understand, plan, and adopt AI-driven solutions in a practical and strategic way.

My professional journey combines academic teaching, applied AI, project management, IT consulting, and research. I am particularly passionate about using AI to solve real-world problems, especially in areas such as computer vision, medical image analysis, intelligent systems, and data-driven decision-making.

I believe that technology becomes truly valuable when it is understandable, usable, and aligned with human and organizational needs. Through teaching, consulting, and research, my goal is to bridge the gap between advanced AI technologies and practical applications that create meaningful impact.

## Academic Projects and Research

Some of my academic and research-related work includes:

### Malaria Cell Detection using Curriculum Learning and Faster R-CNN

Designed and implemented a deep learning pipeline using Faster R-CNN with Detectron2 and curriculum learning strategies to detect malaria-infected cells in microscope images. This project focused on improving detection performance, particularly for images captured using low-cost microscopes.

### Large-Scale Analysis of Citation Bias

Developed a predictive model to analyze citation likelihood based on demographic data. This project explored patterns in academic citation behavior and contributed to broader discussions around fairness and representation in research.

### Efficient Liver Disease Prediction

Built machine learning classifiers for predicting liver disease based on patient biomarkers. This project applied data analysis and classification techniques to support healthcare-related prediction tasks.

### Predicting Safety Risks from Street View Photos

Applied image processing and computer vision techniques to predict safety and crime-related risks using street view images.
